Robots With Their Own Web

The RoboEarth project enables robots to store and share information, using the World-Wide-Web style database. Data includes maps for navigation, task knowledge and object recognition models. The key is collaboration – the cloud can be used by the robots to achieve a common task. (1)

The project brings together researchers from Germany, Netherlands and Spain. (1)

5G Mobile Network

The telecommunications industry is already focused on developing the next-generation cellular communication technology – 5G, even though in many parts of the world 4G LTE networks are still not available. It is expected to dramatically boost speeds of mobile connections, but it could happen in 2018 at the earliest. (2)

Vid Ref: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GEx_d0SjvS0

The Internet of Things

The Internet of Things refers to the idea that every device with and on/off switch can be and will be connected to the Internet. It is predicted that by 2020 they will be over 26 billion. Besides obviously cellphones, it includes coffee makers, washing machines, cars and almost anything you can think of. (3)

Vid Ref: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uEsKZGOxNKw

ConCreTe

ConCreTe researchers put together systems with mechanisms to mimic and enhance human creativity. The goal is to explore it and thus improve computer systems’ inventiveness. Tra-La-Lyrics 2.0 is one of the tools developed, which automatically generates song lyrics, and Brain Poetry generates poems using the person’s alfa brain waves. (4)

Haptic Retargetting

Virtual reality now provides an experience that seems real but one that feels real. This is why Microsoft Research comes up with the idea of a system that uses a limited number of physical props to simulate infinite number of virtual objects. Example: the user will have the impression of stacking countless blocks while in reality it is just moving the same block over and over again. (5)
